## How Sourcing Partners Drive Platform Growth and Revenue

## The Challenge Every Auction Platform Faces

The biggest obstacle for any online auction platform is **supply** — getting enough quality inventory to attract buyers. Without items to bid on, buyers leave. Without buyers, sellers don't list.

Most platforms wait for sellers to find them, register, learn the interface, photograph their items, write descriptions, set prices, and manage the listing process themselves. This is slow, inconsistent, and limits growth to however many sellers are willing to do the work.

**SPC Auctions solves this with Brokers.**

---

## What Is a SPC Auctions Broker?

A Broker is a **professional sourcing partner** — someone who actively goes out into the field, finds companies with equipment to sell, and brings that inventory onto the platform.

Think of it like a real estate agent, but for industrial equipment and commercial assets.

**The seller** is a factory, warehouse, or company that has machinery, vehicles, or inventory they want to sell. They often don't have the time, expertise, or interest in managing an online auction themselves.

**The broker** bridges that gap. They visit the seller's site, assess the equipment, photograph everything, determine fair market values, and submit the items to the platform — ready for auction.

## How It Works

### Step 1 — Lead Generation

The broker identifies a potential seller. This might come from:
- Industry contacts and networking
- Bankruptcy and liquidation announcements
- Company restructuring or relocation news
- Referrals from previous clients
- Cold outreach to businesses upgrading equipment

The broker logs the lead in their SPC Auctions portal with the company name, contact details, and notes about the opportunity.

### Step 2 — Site Visit

The broker schedules and conducts an on-site visit. During the visit, they:
- Inspect and photograph all equipment for sale
- Record specifications (manufacturer, model, year, condition)
- Discuss reserve prices and seller expectations
- Assess logistics (location, accessibility, removal timeline)

### Step 3 — Lot Submission

Back at their desk, the broker submits the items to SPC Auctions:
- **One by one** — using the lot intake form for individual high-value items
- **In bulk** — uploading a spreadsheet for large liquidations with dozens or hundreds of items

Each submission includes photos, descriptions, category, estimated value, and location. The platform's admin team reviews and approves the lots.

### Step 4 — Auction & Sale

Once approved, the items go live on the platform. Buyers across Europe discover them through search, browse by category, or receive notifications. Bidding happens in real time with anti-sniping protection ensuring fair outcomes.

### Step 5 — Settlement

When an item sells, the payment is processed automatically. Commission is deducted, and the seller receives their payout. The broker's contribution is tracked from lead through to sale.

## The Broker Portal

Every broker gets access to their own dedicated portal with tools designed for their workflow:

### Dashboard
At a glance, brokers see their performance:
- Total leads in the pipeline
- Visits scheduled this week
- Lots submitted and approved
- Conversion rate from lead to sale

### Lead Management
A structured pipeline tracks every opportunity from first contact to completion:

| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **New** | Lead identified, contact details recorded |
| **Contacted** | Initial outreach made, interest confirmed |
| **Visit Scheduled** | On-site inspection date set |
| **Visit Completed** | Equipment assessed, photos taken |
| **Lots Submitted** | Items uploaded to the platform |
| **Closed** | Deal complete or opportunity passed |

No leads fall through the cracks. Every opportunity is tracked and measurable.

### Single Lot Intake
For individual high-value items, brokers fill out a detailed form:
- Item title and description
- Equipment category (machinery, vehicles, office equipment, etc.)
- Photos (drag-and-drop upload)
- Estimated value and suggested reserve price
- Location with address and country

### Bulk Intake
For large liquidations — factory closures, warehouse clearances, fleet disposals — brokers upload a spreadsheet with all items at once. The platform validates the data and creates all lots in a single operation.

This is critical for high-volume scenarios where entering 50-200 items one by one would be impractical.

## Why Brokers Are Essential for Growth

### 1. They Solve the Supply Problem

The hardest part of building a marketplace is the chicken-and-egg dilemma: buyers need inventory, but sellers need buyers first. Brokers break this cycle by **actively sourcing inventory** regardless of how many buyers are already on the platform.

Ten active brokers, each bringing 20 lots per month, means **200 new lots every month** — without waiting for a single seller to self-register.

### 2. They Enable High-Value Liquidations

The most profitable auction events are **full liquidations** — an entire factory, warehouse, or fleet being sold at once. These can be 50 to 500 lots in a single event.

Individual sellers won't handle this. They don't have the expertise or the patience. Brokers specialise in exactly this scenario. The bulk intake feature exists specifically to support it.

A single factory liquidation can generate **EUR 100,000-500,000 in gross merchandise value** — and the corresponding platform commission from one event.

### 3. They Expand Geographic Reach Without Offices

Want to operate in Germany, Poland, the Netherlands, Italy, and Romania without opening offices in each country? Onboard local brokers who:
- Know the local market and industry landscape
- Speak the language
- Have existing business contacts
- Understand local regulations and customs

The platform handles everything else — payments, VAT calculation, buyer communication, settlement. The broker just needs to find the inventory.

### 4. They Improve Listing Quality

Self-service seller listings are often poor quality — blurry photos, vague descriptions, unrealistic prices. This leads to fewer bids and lower sale prices.

Brokers are professionals. They know how to:
- Photograph equipment from the right angles with proper lighting
- Write accurate, detailed descriptions that buyers trust
- Set realistic reserve prices based on market knowledge
- Categorise items correctly for search discoverability

Better listings mean more bids, higher hammer prices, and more commission for the platform.

### 5. They Create a Competitive Moat

A network of active brokers is extremely difficult for competitors to replicate. Each broker brings their own contacts, industry expertise, and regional knowledge. The more brokers you have, the more diverse and attractive your inventory becomes, which attracts more buyers, which attracts more sellers and brokers.

This flywheel effect is the same model used by the largest auction houses in Europe.

## Revenue Impact

### Direct Commission Revenue

Every item that sells through the platform generates commission. Brokers bring items that would never exist on the platform otherwise. The math is straightforward:

| Scenario | Lots/Month | Avg. Hammer Price | Monthly GMV | Platform Commission (10%) |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|--------------------------|
| No brokers | Organic only | — | — | Baseline |
| 5 brokers | 100 extra lots | EUR 2,500 | EUR 250,000 | **EUR 25,000** |
| 10 brokers | 200 extra lots | EUR 2,500 | EUR 500,000 | **EUR 50,000** |
| 20 brokers | 400 extra lots | EUR 3,000 | EUR 1,200,000 | **EUR 120,000** |

These numbers are conservative. Industrial equipment auctions regularly see individual lots selling for EUR 5,000-50,000.

### Broker Commission Model

The platform's 10% seller commission can be structured to incentivise brokers:

**Option A — Commission Split**
The existing 10% commission is shared:
- 7% to the platform
- 3% to the broker who sourced the lot

The broker earns money for every item they bring that sells. The platform still earns 7% on inventory it wouldn't have had otherwise. Both parties are incentivised.

**Option B — Higher Total Commission**
When a broker is involved, the total commission increases:
- 10% platform commission + 5% broker commission = 15% total from the seller

This is still competitive — traditional auction houses charge 15-25% seller commission plus 10-18% buyer premium. At 15% total, SPC Auctions remains the more affordable option.

**Option C — Broker Subscription**
Charge brokers a monthly access fee:
- **Starter:** Free — up to 10 lot intakes per month
- **Professional:** EUR 99/month — unlimited intakes, priority review, performance analytics
- **Enterprise:** EUR 299/month — bulk upload, API access, dedicated account manager

Brokers pay because the platform gives them access to a European buyer network they couldn't build on their own.

**These models can be combined.** For example: a EUR 99/month subscription plus 3% commission on sales creates predictable recurring revenue plus performance-based upside.
