Six Reasons This Is Worth More Than EUR 15,000

1. It's a working product — not a prototype
Buyers can search, bid in real-time, and check out. Sellers can list items and track sales. Admins manage the entire operation. This isn't wireframes and promises — it's a running system you can demo today.
2. Enterprise-grade architecture with audit trail
Event sourcing provides a complete, immutable history of every action. CQRS separates reads from writes for performance. Hexagonal architecture makes each service independently testable and replaceable. This is the kind of architecture that large auction houses run on — getting it wrong and fixing it later costs more than building it right from scratch.
3. EU compliance is built in — not bolted on
GDPR data export and deletion, AML screening interfaces, DSA content reporting, PSD2 payment compliance, and VAT calculations for all 27 EU member states. A competitor starting today would spend months just on the compliance layer — and for a platform operating in Europe, this isn't optional, it's legally required.
4. Real-time bidding — the hardest part to build
WebSocket-based live bidding with anti-sniping protection, proxy bidding, bid masking, and automatic auction extensions. This is the most technically complex component of any auction platform, and it works end-to-end. Building and debugging real-time bidding alone typically takes 2–3 months of senior developer time.

One Auction Day Pays for the Platform
